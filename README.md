# Decentralized Training Fellowship Lab

This repository documents my personal learning, research, experiments, and public contributions during the 2026 Flow Research Decentralized Training Fellowship.

It is a personal research repository and is not an official Flow Research repository. Internal fellowship materials, private discussions, confidential information, credentials, and unpublished organisational work are not included.

## Fellowship Focus

The fellowship explores how machine-learning models can be trained across distributed and independently controlled machines without requiring all data and compute to be controlled by one central authority.

Topics include:

- Centralized and distributed training
- Federated learning
- FedAvg and FedProx
- Non-IID data
- Client failures
- Byzantine participants
- Robust aggregation
- Gradient compression
- Gossip learning
- Decentralized averaging
- Evaluation and reproducibility

## Candidate Research Direction

My broader research interest is secure collaborative fraud-model training across financial institutions.

A possible focused research question is:

> How does non-IID financial data affect the robustness of cross-silo federated fraud-detection models when one or more participating institutions submit malicious model updates?

This research direction is provisional and will be refined with mentor feedback.
